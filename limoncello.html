Limoncello Recipe

Off the records, Limoncello tastes amazing. Dr. Swanson said it himself, it's the "nectar of the gods". As I am not of legal drinking age (yet), the following recipe was not tested by yours truly, rather my family members. It has received the seal of approval by multiple Italians, including experts on Limoncello. This recipe has an approximate calorie count of 110 Kcal.

Ingredients

  • 5 Large Organic Lemons
  • 500ml Vodka 50% ABV
  • 350g Sugar
  • 350ml Water

Steps

1. Wash the lemons under the tap, using a sponge to ensure all contaminants are removed from the peel, then dry with a kitchen towel

2. Peel the lemons with a potato peeler, discarding the inner white part of the peel. The remaining peels should only contain zest and the yellow inner part.

3. Pour the vodka and lemon peels into a resealable glass container (such as a jar), and let sit in a dark and cool place for 30 days.

4. After 30 days have passed, pour the water and sugar into a pot and bring to a boil. Once boiling, pour the resulting syrup into a glass container to cool.

5. Once cool, mix the syrup into the vodka and lemon peel container, and let sit in a dark and cool place for 40 days.

6. After 40 days have passed, shake the container then filter the liquor using a strainer or cloth, then place it into a bottle.

How To Conserve

You can conserve the Limoncello you made for quite long in a cool and dry place, such as a fridge or freezer. As the liquor contains 30%+ ABV and sugar, it will not freeze. If you notice any alteration in the liquor (such as discoloration, separation, etc.), do not consume.
